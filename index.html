About the Community Patrol Program

Black Wolf Patrol is a community-focused patrol program currently in development and preparing for formal licensing in the State of California.

Our mission

To provide structured, professional patrol coverage designed specifically for homeowner associations seeking consistent visibility, parking observation, and documented oversight — without the expense of full-time on-site guard staffing.

We are equally committed to building positive, professional relationships within the communities we serve so that residents feel safe, supported, and confident in their surroundings.

Patrol model

Optimized for communities that don’t require constant on-site presence. Patrols occur at varied, randomized times and can be scaled based on community needs.

  • Structured security patrol
  • Visible marked vehicle presence
  • Documented observations and reporting
  • On-request response when issues arise

Partnership-Driven Community Patrol

Black Wolf Patrol was established to provide structured, professional patrol services designed specifically for residential homeowner associations. Our approach enhances visibility and reassurance without the expense of full-time on-site guard staffing.

We work in close partnership with HOA boards and management teams to ensure patrol coverage aligns with community priorities and expectations.

Founded on Public Safety Experience

Black Wolf Patrol was founded by individuals with experience in law enforcement and public safety. That background informs our structured approach to patrol operations, communication, and documentation standards.

While we are a private patrol service — we operate with professionalism, accountability, and respect for every community we serve.

What We Offer

Patrol Coverage

Incident Reporting

Documented incident summaries and timely notifications to management as needed.

FAQ

Quick answers HOA managers typically ask.

When do services start?

Services begin upon completion of California licensing requirements and final service agreement setup.

Are patrol times always at night?

No — patrols occur at randomized times throughout the week and can include daytime or nighttime coverage.

Can the program scale up if needed?

Yes — coverage can increase based on community needs, seasonal trends, or specific concerns.

Do you handle towing or citations?

We focus on parking observation and documentation, then report findings to HOA management for follow-up.
